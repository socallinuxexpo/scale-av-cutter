== Editor Workflow

Editors perform the initial identification and technical marking of the stream.
Editors claim an *entire room*, not individual talks. Note that speakers may not show
up or a talk may occur at a different time than scheduled.

[CAUTION]
====
*Coordinate with other volunteers before editing* so that no one accidentally
overwrites anyone's work.
====

=== Step by Step

. *Select the room* from the Status List that contains the talk you will edit.
. *Select the talk* you will edit.
. *Use the embedded YouTube player* to find the portion of the video matching the provided title and abstract.
. *Confirm the metadata* — make sure the video content matches the title and abstract. +
+
[WARNING]
====
Automation pulls metadata directly into the YouTube description.
If the mapping is incorrect, *public metadata will be permanently flawed upon upload.*
====

. *Set Video Start and Video End* — find the precise start and end points of the presentation and use the *dropper tool* to capture timestamps directly into the input fields.

. *Select the Thumbnail* — navigate to a representative frame (typically the title slide or a clear speaker shot; avoid boring title slides and prefer something visually exciting) and use the dropper tool to set the thumbnail.

. *Add Notes* — this is critical for saving reviewers time. Sign off your notes, because if someone else edits the entry, your display name may be overwritten. +
+
[IMPORTANT]
====
You *must* click the *"Save notes"* button or your note will not be saved.
====

. *Set the Edit Status:*

[cols="1,3", options="header"]
|===
|Status |Meaning

|*Done*
|Content is complete and ready for review. Automatically transitions the room to
*Needs Review* for the Reviewer. Talk appears *Green-Grey*.

|*Incomplete*
|Part of the talk is usable, but it is not a standard full recording. Use the Notes field
to describe which parts are usable. Automatically transitions to *Needs Review*.
These talks can sometimes be uploaded with a note rather than discarded.

|*Unusable*
|Select when: the audio or video is persistently corrupted, or the speaker is
entirely out of frame. Automatically transitions to *Needs Review*. Talk appears *Yellow-Grey*.
|===
